Great Britain-China Dialogue Program

The Great Britain China Dialogue Program (GBCDP) promotes mutual trust and understanding between the UK and China. GBCDP works for shared prosperity through dialogue.

GBCDP is a government program.

Run workshop with GBCDP

17 May 2019 — News story

GBCDP has developed a workshop that helps Chinese students to settle into their new school/university more easily as well as develop a global perspective for all participants. This workshop focuses on one of the hottest topic: Internet Censorship. This workshop can be run by staff or students.

Workshop preparation:

  • Multiple age-appropriate magazines/publications.

  • A similar number of Chinese and non-Chinese participants.

  • Pens in different colours.

Workshop structure:

Separate Chinese and non-Chinese students into two groups ask participants to circle out content that they think would be censored in China. During this process, you can ask them why they think one particular element would be censored. The session normally lasts from 10-20mins depending on the quantity of work and the depth you are planning to go into.

After the section, count the number of circles then compare two sets of data produced by both groups. Start to discuss why there is a big or small difference. The discussion can focus on one particular content, overall difference or both. Through these discussions, participants will gain a deeper understanding of other cultural views.

Read more about GBCDP

Almost a century ago, Robert Moses’ flyovers physically separated people from different class and ethnical backgrounds. In today’s society, such physical separation has been strongly criticised and slowly erased. As a result, we are constantly seeing and meeting people from different backgrounds. Even with that, sometimes we still find it difficult to communicate with each other: often this is not because of language barriers, but a difference in the social and political environments people grow up in.

Today, our society is transforming itself into the digital world. New separations arise: some of them are separating us at a country level (for example, the Great Firewall of China), some of them are separating us on an individual basis (for example, the filter bubble phenomenon).





Zeyang Zheng

Founder of GBCDP
